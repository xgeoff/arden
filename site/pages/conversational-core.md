### 🌤️ Adjective Inflection Forms

> **Rule recap:**
>
> * Adjectives follow the noun
> * Adjectives are gender-neutral
> * Comparative and superlative forms are optional and semantic
> * Analytic forms (`mor`, `seel`) may always substitute for synthetic forms

| safe                 | **sur**      | surel      | surar             | surestel             | Physical & emotional       |

### 🧩 Analytic Comparison (Preferred in Casual Speech)

While synthetic forms are valid, **spoken Arden strongly favors analytic comparison**, especially for abstract adjectives.

| Meaning         | Form             |
| --------------- | ---------------- |
| more busy       | **mor prethel**  |
| most busy       | **seel prethel** |
| more exhausting | **mor dranel**   |
| most ridiculous | **seel farrel**  |

Synthetic forms remain available for:

* emphasis
* poetic or formal registers
* short evaluative statements

### 🗣️ Usage Examples (Validated)

* *Me prethel solinor.*  
  → I am busy today.

* *Tu lirel toravel, kaa?*  
  → Are you free this weekend?

* *Durne surel.*  
  → Drive safe.

* *Grelvel nareth.*  
  → Miserable night.

* *Mor dranel solinor.*  
  → More exhausted today.

* *Seel farrel idea.*  
  → Most ridiculous idea.

### 🗣️ Discourse & Idioms

Discourse particles and the idiom strategy avoid bloating the lexicon:

* **Selai / Olven / Morai / Silven** already serve idiomatic functions.
* Translate English idioms by intent, not literal metaphor. Prefer particles, aspect markers, and the existing verbs.
* Canonize an idiom only when it becomes a stable Arden phrase shared across conversations.
* “Be right back” → `Me vare, veran soon.` (verb + soon particle); “On my way!” → `Me vare rin!`.

Optional idiomatic coverage:

| jinx (verb) | **kornaa**   | verb        | Oversees superstition and avoidance   |
