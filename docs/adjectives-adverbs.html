Adjectives: Always end in -el, come after the noun, and are fully gender-neutral. Comparative and superlative forms use -ar and -estel, or the helper words mor and seel. Here are two examples showing how mor and seel work in practice:

Example 1: Using mor (‚Äúmore‚Äù)

English: The mountain is taller. Arden: An duran mor torenel.

  • duran = mountain

  • torenel = tall

  • mor places emphasis on comparative, instead of torenar.

  • Adjectives always follow the noun they modify

Example 2: Using seel (‚Äúmost‚Äù)

English: She is the most beautiful girl. Arden: Mila es an caalin seel lumnel.

  • mila = she

  • caalin = girl

  • lumnel = beautiful (from lumne, dream/light)

  • seel makes ‚Äúmost beautiful‚Äù instead of lumnelestel.

  • Arden words must feel elegant, poetic, or grounded

üìò Arden Adverb Formation Principles

In Arden:

  • Adverbs follow the verb directly

  • Many adverbs are formed from:

    • Adjective root + position (e.g., quick ‚Üí quickly)

    • Or via dedicated roots (especially for time/frequency/logical concepts)

  • We'll assign unique roots when needed ‚Äî not just mechanically derived
