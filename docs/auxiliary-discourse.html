Arden Auxiliary & Discourse System

Minimal Conversational Grammar

1. Design Principles

  1. Arden uses suffix tense on verbs and particles for question, modality, aspect, and discourse.
  2. Declarative word order is preserved in all constructions.
  3. The copula is optional in short descriptive clauses, but Arden also has an explicit copula: es.
  4. Modal and discourse particles are invariant and optional.
  5. The system is optimized for spoken, everyday language.

2. Interrogatives (Questions)

2.1 Yes / No Questions

  • Particle: kaa
  • Position: sentence-final

Yes/no questions are formed by appending kaa to a declarative clause. No inversion or verb movement occurs.

[statement], kaa?

Examples

Tu vare toran, kaa?
“Are you going home?”

Orel olven, kaa?
“Is everything okay?”

2.2 Open Questions (Wh-type)

  • Particle: ke
  • Position: clause-initial

ke introduces an open question (“what”, “where”, “why”, “how”, etc.). Specific meaning is inferred from context and the verb.

Ke [statement]?

Examples

Ke tu mentaa?
“What are you thinking?”

Ke mile vare?
“Where are we going?”

2.3 Topic / Suggestion Prompt

  • Particle: ken
  • Position: clause-initial

ken introduces a topic, suggestion, or soft prompt (“what about…”, “how about…”).

Examples

Ken toravel?
“What about Saturday?”

Ken kareth?
“How about coffee?”

3. Aspect Markers

Aspect markers follow the tense-marked verb and are optional.

ParticleMeaning
rinongoing / currently
senhabitual / these days
prenabout to / imminent
jureljust / recently

Examples

Me labore rin.
“I am working.”

Tu labore sen.
“You have been busy lately.”

Me vare pren.
“I am about to go.”

4. Modal Auxiliaries

Modal auxiliaries precede the verb and do not inflect.

ModalMeaning
kancan / could
shalshould
wudwould
melivmight

Examples

Tu kan helpe me?

Mile shal vare merk, kaa?

Me wud stae toran solenya.

5. Negation

  • Particle: no
  • Position: immediately before the verb or predicate

Negation is expressed with no. No auxiliary support is required.

Examples

Me no agree.
“I do not agree.”

No relen.
“It does not matter.”

Tu no vare solinor.
“You are not going today.”

6. Copula Strategy (Zero Copula)

Arden allows zero copula, but it also has an explicit copula: es. Use es when you want a clear linking verb; omit it freely in short, obvious descriptive clauses.

Examples

Me morv solinor.
“I am exhausted today.”

Orel olven.
“Everything is okay.”

Melun namor es Kareth.
“My name is Kareth.”

7. Discourse & Social Particles

These particles operate at the sentence or conversational level.

ParticleMeaning

Examples

Selai.

Olven, jaan.

Dolvai.

8. Ellipsis Rule (Casual Speech)

In informal speech, subject pronouns may be omitted when context makes them recoverable.

Examples

Vare rin, kaa?
“You coming?”

Labore rin solinor?
“Busy today?”

Vare rin! “On my way!”

10. Idiom Strategy

Rather than inventing literal calques, Arden translates the intended meaning of English idioms while keeping the sound and syntax consistent with the auxiliary/discourse system.

  • Default: render idioms with existing particles, tense-marked verbs, and aspect markers (e.g., Selai. for “No worries,” Olven. for “All good,” Me vare rin! for “On my way!”).
  • Only canonize a new idiom if it becomes a stable Arden phrase used across conversations (like Selai, Olven, Morai).
  • Use modal/auxiliary particles plus suggested verbs to express English idiomatic actions (e.g., “Be right back” → Me vare, veran soon. using veran = return/soon, Me + vare + aspect marker).
  • When an idiom relies on a specific discourse tone (surprise, frustration, encouragement), prefer the closest existing discourse particle (Dolvai, Selai, etc.) instead of molding new vocabulary.

This keeps Arden idioms natural, minimal, and aligned with the rest of the grammatical system.

9. Summary

With a small, phonologically consistent set of particles, Arden supports:

  • Yes/no and open questions
  • Aspect and modality
  • Polite requests and suggestions
  • Negation without auxiliary verbs
  • Natural ellipsis and conversational flow

These particles work alongside Arden's tense-marked verbs and optional copula, preserving simple clause order while keeping the language expressive.
